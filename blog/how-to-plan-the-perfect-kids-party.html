How to Plan the Perfect Kids' Party

A stress-free guide from the Hooray HQ team

Planning a kids' party can feel like a lot — but it doesn't have to be. With a simple plan and a few statement touches, you can throw a celebration the birthday boy or girl will talk about for weeks (we've seen it happen). Here's how we'd approach it.

1. Start with a theme (4–6 weeks out)

A theme makes every other decision easier — colours, cake, games, and decorations all flow from it. Ask your little one what they're into right now: dinosaurs, mermaids, a favourite colour, space, sports. You don't need to go overboard; even a strong two- or three-colour palette reads as "themed" once the balloons are up.

2. Lock in the date, time & guest list (3–4 weeks out)

  • Timing: for younger kids, late morning or early afternoon (after a nap) works best. Keep it to 2–2.5 hours.
  • Numbers: a rough rule is to invite as many friends as the child's age, plus a few — it keeps things manageable.
  • Send invites with the theme so parents can dress the part.

3. Plan the space & the "wow" moment (2–3 weeks out)

Every great party has one spot that makes kids go "wow" as they walk in — usually a backdrop behind the cake or party table. This is where balloons do the heavy lifting. A balloon garland or freestanding arch in your theme colours instantly lifts the whole room, and doubles as the perfect photo spot.

Short on time? Our Grab & Go range lets you pick your colours and collect a ready-made garland, column, or arch. Want it fully styled and installed? Our Custom & Themed service brings the whole look to life and sets it up for you.

4. Sort food & activities (1–2 weeks out)

  • Food: keep it simple and grazing-friendly — fruit, savoury bites, and one show-stopping cake.
  • Activities: 2–3 is plenty. A craft, a classic game (pass the parcel never fails), and free play.
  • Party favours: a small thank-you bag sends everyone home happy.

5. The day before

Prep food, charge your camera, and set up your backdrop and balloons so the morning is calm. If we're installing for you, we'll handle this part — you just show up and enjoy it.
